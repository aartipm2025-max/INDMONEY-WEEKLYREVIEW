### Fee Explainer (Exit Load)
- An exit load is a charge levied by mutual fund houses when an investor redeems their investment before a specified period.
- The exit load is typically a percentage of the Net Asset Value (NAV) or the redemption amount.
- The holding period for which an exit load is applicable varies across different mutual fund schemes.
- Exit loads are designed to discourage short-term trading and encourage long-term investment in mutual funds.
- The specific percentage and tenure for exit loads are disclosed in the Scheme Information Document (SID) and Key Information Memorandum (KIM) of each mutual fund scheme.
- This charge is deducted directly from the redemption proceeds at the time of sale.

**Sources:** https://www.indmoney.com/mutual-funds/exit-load | https://www.sebi.gov.in/legal/handbook-of-sebi-guidelines/chapter-40-mutual-funds

### Fee Explainer (Exit Load)
- An exit load is a charge levied by a mutual fund scheme when an investor redeems their investment before a specified period.
- The purpose of an exit load is to discourage short-term trading and encourage long-term investment in mutual funds.
- The exit load percentage typically decreases as the holding period increases and becomes zero after a certain tenure.
- The specific exit load structure, including the percentage and the holding period for its applicability, is detailed in the scheme's offer document.
- Exit loads are deducted from the redemption amount at the time of sale.
- Regulatory bodies like SEBI prescribe guidelines for the imposition of exit loads by mutual fund houses.
